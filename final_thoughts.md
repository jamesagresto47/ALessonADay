# Final Thoughts

## How you handled the question

You did a great job of trying to understand the problem and breaking it down. You were persistent in your efforts to solve the problem, even when you encountered difficulties. You were also open to feedback and willing to try new approaches.

## What you could've done better

While you were persistent, you sometimes got stuck on a single approach for too long. It is important to be able to recognize when an approach is not working and to be able to pivot to a new one. Additionally, you could have been more proactive in asking for clarification on the requirements of the problem.

## What to look out for next time

For future interviews, focus on the following:

*   **Problem Comprehension:** Before writing any code, make sure you fully understand the problem. Restate the problem in your own words to the interviewer to confirm your understanding.
*   **Multiple Approaches:** Brainstorm multiple ways to solve the problem. Discuss the trade-offs (time complexity, space complexity) of each approach with your interviewer.
*   **Communication:** Clearly communicate your thought process. Explain what you are trying to do and why. If you get stuck, explain what you have tried and what you are thinking of trying next.
*   **Testing:** Think about edge cases and how you would test your solution.
